1. Base Governance Protocol Model (9 Layers)

A reusable abstraction for any polity: from cities to nation-states. The model is expressed as nine composable layers that define who participates, how decisions are made, how resources move, and how rights are protected.

Layers 1–3
Actors, Identity & Representation

Define the governance domain, attach digital identities, and wire them into direct or delegated representation models (e.g., liquid democracy).

Actors & roles Digital ID Delegation graphs
Layers 4–6
Decision Flow & Policy Execution

Model the lifecycle from idea → proposal → deliberation → vote, and connect outcomes to budgets, laws, and program execution.

Proposal lifecycle Voting protocols Treasury hooks
Layers 7–9
Constitution, Infra & Resilience

Encode rights, checks, and emergency powers; define the chain / infra layer; and design for accessibility, recovery, and adversarial conditions.

Rights models Infra primitives Threat modeling

2. Canada Mappings · Federal · Provincial · Municipal

The same 9-layer model is applied three times: to Canada’s federal government, provincial governments, and municipal (city) governments. Each mapping shows what can live on-chain, off-chain, or in hybrid.

Federal
Parliament, Charter & National Systems

House of Commons, Senate, Prime Minister & Cabinet, federal courts, Elections Canada, national departments, and the Charter are wired into the protocol model.

→ View federal mapping
Provincial
Health, Education & Land

Unicameral legislatures, Premiers, ministries, and provincial courts drive high-touch services like health, education, and land use: ideal mid-scale protocol targets.

→ View provincial mapping
Municipal
Cities, Zoning & Services

Mayors, councils, zoning, permits, budgets, and local services are modeled as protocol flows, with strong emphasis on participatory budgeting and planning.

→ View municipal mapping

4. Roadmap · From Diagrams to Code

The current repo is conceptual and architectural. The next step is to turn these abstractions into concrete protocol designs and, eventually, deployed governance tooling.
