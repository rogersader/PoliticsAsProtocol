Overview

Turning Canada’s governance into a protocol stack.

This repository treats politics like a distributed system: actors, rules, decisions, and safeguards expressed as a layered, composable protocol that can run on-chain or hybrid.

Inside: a reusable 9-layer governance model, plus concrete mappings for Canada’s federal, provincial, and municipal systems.

1. Base governance protocol model (9 layers)

A generic framework for expressing any political system as a protocol. It defines who participates, how decisions are made, how resources move, and how rights are enforced.

Layers 1–3
Governance domain, identity, representation

Define actors, rights, and roles; attach digital identities; and wire them into direct voting and delegated representation (e.g., liquid democracy).

Actors & roles Digital credentials Delegation graphs
Layers 4–6
Proposals, voting, and policy execution

Model the lifecycle from idea to binding decision, and connect outcomes to laws, budgets, and program execution using on-chain or hybrid components.

Proposal lifecycle Voting protocol Treasury flows
Layers 7–9
Constitution, infrastructure, resilience

Encode rights and constraints, define the underlying infrastructure (chains, clients, APIs), and design for accessibility, security, and adversarial conditions.

Rights & oversight Infra primitives Threat models

Detailed description lives in governance-model.md.

2. Canada mappings: federal, provincial, municipal

The 9-layer model is applied three times: to the federal system, to provinces, and to municipal (city) governments. Each mapping shows what can realistically be on-chain, off-chain, or hybrid.

Federal
Parliament, Charter, national systems

House of Commons, Senate, Prime Minister and Cabinet, Elections Canada, federal courts, and the Charter mapped into the protocol layers.

View federal mapping →
Provincial
Health, education, land, municipalities

Unicameral legislatures, Premiers, ministries, and provincial courts wired into the model, with emphasis on high-touch services and provincial autonomy.

View provincial mapping →
Municipal
Cities, zoning, services, daily life

Mayors, councils, zoning, permitting, local budgets, and service delivery modelled as protocol flows with participatory overlays.
